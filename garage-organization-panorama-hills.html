Frequently Asked Questions — Garage Organization in Panorama Hills

What makes Panorama Hills garages particularly challenging?

Panorama Hills is one of Calgary's most family-dense communities, with large households often including multiple children in different sports. A single household can have hockey gear in three sizes, soccer equipment, bikes for every family member, and camping supplies. Add multi-generational living — which is common in the community — and garages fill up with items from two generations sharing one space.

How do you handle sports equipment for multiple kids?

We create per-sport dedicated zones on the walls, installed at heights appropriate for each user. Hockey bags go on heavy-duty floor hooks, helmets on wall racks, and sticks in a floor rack. Soccer balls, cleats, and smaller gear go in labelled bins. Each sport gets its own clearly marked zone so the system is self-maintaining — kids can use it independently before and after practice.

We have a multi-generational household with items from two families — can you help?

Yes. Multi-generational households are common in Panorama Hills and we design systems with clearly defined zones for each generation. This typically means colour-coded labels, divider shelving, or separate wall sections so grandparents' tools, parents' gear, and kids' sports equipment all have defined, respected spaces with no overlap.
