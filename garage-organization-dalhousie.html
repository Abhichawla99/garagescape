About Dalhousie — NW Calgary's Active Family Community

Dalhousie was developed through the 1970s and into the 1980s, giving it a housing stock of bungalows and two-storey split-levels on well-established lots. The neighbourhood sits conveniently close to the Dalhousie CTrain station on the red line — one of the reasons it has attracted and kept commuter-friendly residents who work downtown or across Calgary without needing a car every day.

Access in and out of Dalhousie is straightforward: Shaganappi Trail connects you south toward the core, 14 St NW runs the eastern edge toward Brentwood and the University of Calgary, and Northland Drive puts you a few minutes from Northland Village Mall and the rest of NW Calgary's commercial corridor. For a community developed fifty years ago, the bones are excellent.

The community skews toward long-term residents — empty nesters who have been in their homes since the kids were small, and families with teenagers deep into hockey, skiing, and rugby. Both groups share the same garage problem: decades of accumulated gear, seasonal items that have stopped being rotated, and a single 9-foot-wide garage door that was optimistic for two cars even in 1978.

The CTrain access is a double-edged sword for Dalhousie garages. It is one of the neighbourhood's biggest selling points — but when you commute by train every day, the garage quietly transforms from a parking space into a very expensive outdoor storage locker. We help you either reclaim it for parking or simply make it work as proper organized storage, depending on what you actually need.

Dalhousie Garage Organization — Common Questions

Why do Dalhousie homeowners have so much trouble fitting a car in their garage?

Dalhousie was built in the 1970s and 80s when the standard double garage door was 9 feet wide. With modern vehicles, one car takes up most of that space. Add sports gear and seasonal storage and the car ends up permanently outside. We reorganize the space so the car gets its spot back — or at minimum so you could park inside when January hits -35°C.

I take the CTrain every day. My garage is basically a storage locker. Can you fix that?

This is almost a Dalhousie cliche, and yes — we fix it weekly. When you do not park in the garage, it fills up fast without any system. We design storage that maximizes capacity while keeping clear pathways and logical zones, so that on cold January mornings, parking inside is a realistic option again.

We have skiing, hockey, camping, and biking gear all competing for space. Where do we start?

Seasonal rotation is the answer. Not all of that gear needs to be accessible at once. We divide your storage into active (current season) and dormant (off-season) zones. Active gear lives at eye level on wall panels and hooks. Dormant gear goes into overhead platforms or upper shelves. You stop digging for things and start reaching for them.

How long does a Dalhousie garage organization project take?

Garages with significant seasonal gear accumulation typically take 5 to 7 hours. If the garage has decades of mixed items from multiple family phases, a full day is more realistic. We provide an accurate estimate at the free quote stage so you can plan your day accordingly.

Can you help with a garage that has sports gear mixed with general household storage?

Mixed-use garages are the norm in Dalhousie. We create clear zones: a sports wall for active gear, an overhead area for seasonal and bulky items, a utility wall for garden tools and household overflow, and floor space reclaimed for parking. Each zone is labelled so the system maintains itself.

Do you serve other NW Calgary communities near Dalhousie?

Yes — we regularly work in Brentwood, Varsity, Charleswood, Hawkwood, Edgemont, and other NW Calgary communities. If you are within a few kilometres of Dalhousie Station, we cover your neighbourhood. Call to confirm your specific address.
