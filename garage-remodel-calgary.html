The 5-Step Garage Remodel Process

1

Consultation and Layout Planning

We start with a free consultation, either on-site or by phone. We ask about the garage dimensions, what needs to be stored, how many vehicles, and what the household wants the finished space to look like. From this we draft a storage layout and floor treatment plan before we book the job. You approve the plan before any work starts.

2

Full Declutter and Sort

The garage is cleared. Every item is sorted: keep, donate, recycle, or dispose. This step is done with you or another adult present so keep/dispose decisions reflect what the household actually needs. Items for donation go to Calgary charities. We remove everything that is not staying before moving to the next step.

3

Deep Clean and Surface Preparation

With the garage fully clear, we deep clean from top to bottom. Floor stains are treated and degreased. Walls are wiped down. The floor is prepared for coating. This step is critical because storage systems installed on a clean, sealed floor stay stable over time, while systems installed on an untreated floor can shift as the concrete absorbs moisture seasonally.

4

Storage System and Fixture Installation

The storage system goes in according to the pre-approved layout plan. Wall shelving is anchored into studs, not just drywall. Overhead racks are secured to ceiling joists. Hooks, utility mounts, and organization accessories are installed. We test every installation before proceeding. A shelf that is going to hold 200 lbs of seasonal gear needs to be properly anchored.

5

Floor Coating, Final Organization, and Walkthrough

The floor coating is applied after all installation is complete so there are no boot marks or scuffs from installation work. While the floor cures, items from the keep pile are placed in their designated zones and labelled. Once the floor is ready for foot traffic, we do a full walkthrough with you to explain the system and ensure you are satisfied with the result.

Calgary-Specific Remodel Considerations

Calgary's climate and housing stock create specific variables that matter for a garage remodel. Here is what you need to know.

Heated vs. Unheated Garages

Calgary has a significant number of heated attached garages, particularly in newer SE, SW, and NW communities. Heated garages are ideal for remodels because they allow the full range of floor coating products, keep storage systems at more stable temperatures (reducing material expansion and contraction), and make the space comfortable to work in year-round.

Unheated garages, more common in older NE and NW communities and in detached garage situations, require some additional consideration. We use cold-weather-tolerant floor products, ensure that storage anchor points account for seasonal concrete movement, and recommend against certain product types that do not perform well in extreme cold. We work in unheated garages year-round and have adapted our materials accordingly.

Attached vs. Detached Garages

Attached garages in Calgary are directly connected to the home and are often accessed frequently throughout the day. The remodel layout for an attached garage prioritizes high-frequency access items near the door and parking clearance. Sports gear, footwear storage, and utility hooks near the interior door are typically part of the plan.

Detached garages are often used more heavily for tools and project work, and less as a daily pass-through. The remodel layout for a detached garage typically emphasizes workshop zones, project storage, and robust shelving for seasonal items. Floor treatment matters more in detached garages because they are more likely to have years of unaddressed oil and grime buildup.

Seasonal Storage Demands

Calgary homeowners store more seasonal items per household than most other Canadian cities. Winter tires (a near-universal requirement), ski and snowboard gear, Stampede gear, camping equipment, outdoor furniture, and in many cases a second set of outdoor tools for both summer and winter. A Calgary garage remodel that does not account for this seasonal volume will run out of storage capacity faster than the homeowner expects.

Our remodel plans always include a seasonal storage audit. We ask what you store each season, how often you access it, and how it needs to be organized. Overhead ceiling racks are frequently the answer for seasonal items that are accessed only a few times a year. Wall shelving handles the rest.

Garage Dimensions Across Calgary Communities

Garage dimensions vary significantly across Calgary neighbourhoods. Older NW and NE communities from the 1970s-1980s often have single-car or 1.5-car garages with lower ceilings. Homes from the 1990s typically have standard double-car attached garages. Newer SE and SW communities built after 2005 often have large attached double or triple garages with higher ceilings suited to full overhead rack systems.

The remodel scope and product selection changes with the dimensions. A remodel in a Brentwood single-car needs completely different shelving choices than a remodel in a Cranston triple. We design for the actual space, not a generic template.

Garage Remodel Calgary FAQ

What is included in a garage remodel?

A GarageScape remodel includes all five steps: consultation and layout planning, full declutter and sort, deep clean, storage system installation, and floor coating. The result is a garage that looks and functions completely differently. We design the layout before work starts so you know what you are getting.

How is a remodel different from just getting organized?

Organization alone tidies up what you have. A remodel starts from a cleared, clean space and builds a designed system from scratch. The floor gets treated. The storage is planned for your specific household. The result is a designed space, not just a tidier one.

What about heated garages in Calgary?

Heated garages are ideal. They allow a wider range of floor coating products, maintain more stable conditions for storage systems, and make the space usable year-round. We ask about heating during the consultation to choose the right materials. Unheated garages are also fully supported with appropriate cold-weather products.

How long does a garage remodel take?

Most remodels take one to two days. Day one handles the declutter, clean, and storage installation. If an epoxy floor coating is applied, we may return for a short follow-up once the floor has cured. Smaller garages can complete in a single day start to finish.

What is the ROI of a garage remodel in Calgary?

A remodelled garage adds real value at resale. Calgary buyers notice garages and a finished, organized space with a treated floor consistently draws positive comments during showings. Beyond resale, the daily practical value of a functional garage in Alberta's climate is significant. Most clients recover the cost quickly in avoided frustration and better use of their space.
