Three Kinds of South Calgary Garage — One Crew That Knows All Three

The Rear-Lane Garage

McKenzie Towne, Cranston, Copperfield, New Brighton, and much of Mahogany were built new-urbanism style: front porches facing the street, garages tucked on the back lane. A detached 16x20 or 18x20 lane garage is often the household's only storage — no basement crawl space overflow, no shed. Floor space is the scarcest resource, so we design upward: overhead ceiling racks over the hood line, full-height wall track, and tight corner shelving that leaves room to actually park.

The Lake-Community Garage

South Calgary holds most of the city's private lake communities — Lake Bonavista, the original from the late 1960s, plus Mahogany, Auburn Bay, Chaparral, Sundance, and McKenzie Lake. Lake access means a second gear inventory most Calgary garages never see: paddleboards, kayaks, life jackets, fishing rods, and beach wagons all summer, then skates, sticks, and snow shovels for lake ice in winter. Without a system, that inventory swallows a bay by August. We build it into racks and zones instead.

The Estate Double and Triple

Signal Hill, Discovery Ridge, Evergreen, Lake Bonavista's estate streets, and newer deep-south communities like Legacy, Walden, and Silverado carry oversized doubles and triples — and the same third-bay problem we see in Aspen Woods and Springbank Hill: one full bay quietly converts to storage while a vehicle winters outside. Custom cabinets, slatwall, and overhead storage reclaim the bay without giving up a single bin of what's in it.

Fish Creek on the Doorstep

Nearly every South Calgary community touches or borders Fish Creek Provincial Park — one of the largest urban parks in Canada — and it shows in the garages: bikes for the whole family, strollers, cross-country skis, paddle gear for Sikome Lake days. Gear that gets used weekly needs grab-and-go wall storage at the door, not a pile behind the second car. We stage garages around how often things leave the building.

Chinooks, Salt, and the South-Facing Slab

Every chinook drags meltwater, road salt, and gravel off Macleod, Deerfoot, and Stoney Trail commutes onto your concrete, then refreezes it into the surface overnight. Over a few winters that cycle pits and dusts a bare slab. A proper clean-out and floor treatment — and getting clutter off the floor so it can dry — is the difference between a slab that lasts and one that spalls. Our garage cleaning crews deal with the salt line every spring.

The Two-Swap Calendar

South Calgary garages run on a rhythm: April–May, winter tires and skates out, bikes and lake gear forward before beaches open; September–October, the reverse, before the first hard freeze locks everything where it sits. We build labelled seasonal zones so each swap takes twenty minutes — and we'll do the swap for you as part of a spring cleanout or fall reset.
