Our Mount Royal Custom Cabinet Process

1

Measure & Design

We measure the garage precisely — walls, ceiling height, door swings, windows, and the man-door to the lane — and catalogue what needs a home. Heritage garages hide surprises (angled rooflines, old workbenches, non-square corners), and a made-to-measure design accounts for all of them before anything is built.

2

Declutter & Sort

Before installation we clear the backlog — decades of paint tins, outgrown bikes, and boxes that migrated out of the house. Keep, donate, sell, dispose, handled with calm, judgment-free guidance, and usable items routed to donation rather than the landfill.

3

Floor First

If your project includes a floor coating, it goes in while the garage is empty. In an older Mount Royal slab that often means grinding, crack repair, and a polyaspartic or epoxy system chosen for a detached, unheated space so the finish holds through freeze-thaw.

4

Install Cabinets, Slatwall & Overhead

Full-height cabinet towers for closed storage, slatwall for the gear you reach for weekly, and overhead platforms for seasonal bins — all planned so the vehicle still parks and every door and window still opens. In compact garages, going vertical is what makes room reappear.

5

Walkthrough & Handover

We label, walk you through the layout, and leave a garage that stays organized because everything has an obvious place — including a simple plan for swapping summer and winter gear as the seasons turn.

The Mount Royal Garage Challenge — and How We Solve It

Heritage Homes, Modern Storage Needs

Mount Royal was laid out by the CPR in the early 1900s as Calgary's premier address, and many of its garages are nearly as old as the homes. They were sized for a single car and a push mower — not for two SUVs' worth of gear, mountain bikes, and a household's seasonal overflow. Rather than fight the footprint, we build up: full-height cabinetry and wall systems that give a small heritage garage the capacity of one twice its size.

The Detached-Garage Reality

Because Mount Royal runs on back lanes, the garage is usually a standalone building set away from the house and often unheated. That changes what belongs there. We spec sealed cabinets that shrug off dust and lane grit, floor coatings rated for cold detached slabs, and a layout that keeps the everyday items reachable so you're not trekking across the yard in January for something buried in a corner.

Compact Footprints Reward Custom Work

This is where made-to-measure cabinets genuinely beat the big-box kit. A stock unit assumes a flat, tall, square wall; a 1930s or infill Mount Royal garage rarely offers one. Building to the actual dimensions — around the man-door, under the low run of roofline, past the window — is the difference between a garage that finally parks a car and one that stays a storage room with a door.

Chinooks and Older Concrete

Calgary's chinook freeze-thaw cycling is hard on any slab, and doubly so on older, unheated Mount Royal garage floors that have weathered decades of meltwater, road salt, and gravel. Left bare, they pit, dust, and stain. A properly ground and coated floor seals the concrete, brightens a dim heritage garage, and turns winter cleanup into a quick squeegee.

Privacy and Valuables Handled with Care

Inner-city estate households don't want a rotating cast of trades and open garage doors advertising what's inside. Our white-glove approach means one contact, scheduled visits, protected finishes, and discreet handling of collector vehicles, tools, and gear. When you'd rather keep the process off the street entirely, photo and video quoting lets us plan the whole job before anyone arrives.

Resale in a Heritage Market

Buyers shopping Mount Royal, Elbow Park, and Britannia expect finished, considered spaces — and an original, cluttered garage stands out for the wrong reasons. A clean coated floor and built-in cabinetry photograph as a feature and quietly signal that the whole property has been well kept, a meaningful edge in Calgary's most established estate market.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Garage Cabinets in Mount Royal

Can you install custom garage cabinets in a small heritage Mount Royal garage?

Yes — compact and heritage garages are exactly where custom cabinets earn their keep. Many Upper and Lower Mount Royal homes have older single or narrow double garages with limited floor space, so we build vertically: full-height cabinet towers, wall-mounted slatwall, and overhead storage that free the floor for a vehicle. Because cabinets are made to your exact dimensions rather than bought off a shelf, we can work around low ceilings, side doors, windows, and awkward heritage layouts that stock units never fit.

Do you organize detached rear-lane garages in Mount Royal?

We do — most of Mount Royal is served by back lanes, and detached garages are the norm. A detached garage has its own challenges: it is further from the house, often unheated, and doubles as workshop, seasonal storage, and sometimes a coach-house parking spot. We design these as self-contained systems with sealed cabinets to keep dust and rodents out, clearly zoned tool and seasonal storage, and a floor coating that stands up to an unheated Calgary winter.

What does luxury garage organization cost in Mount Royal?

Every Mount Royal project is quoted individually. A decluttering and organization project starts around $199, while a full transformation with custom cabinetry, slatwall, overhead racks, and an epoxy or polyaspartic floor is a larger investment scoped to your garage's size, condition, and finish level. We always begin with a free, no-obligation consultation and a written quote — no surprises.

Do you offer discreet, white-glove service for high-value homes?

Yes. Mount Royal households value privacy, and we work accordingly: a single point of contact, scheduled appointments rather than a rotating parade of trades, drop cloths and floor protection through the home and driveway, and careful handling of collector vehicles, wine, and valuables. Photo and video quotes are available if you would rather not have anyone on site until the plan is set.

Can you build in wine, sports, and gear storage?

Absolutely. Beyond standard cabinets we plan for the things Mount Royal households actually store — golf clubs and skis for the mountains, road and gravel bikes, hockey and lacrosse gear, seasonal decor, and climate-appropriate storage for items you would rather not leave in the main house. For temperature-sensitive items like wine we advise on placement, since an unheated detached garage swings too much through Calgary's chinooks; conditioned spaces or the attached garage are usually the right home.

Do you also serve Elbow Park, Britannia, and Roxboro?

Yes. Mount Royal sits at the centre of Calgary's inner-city heritage district, and we regularly work the surrounding estate communities — Elbow Park, Britannia, Roxboro, Bel-Aire, Rideau Park, and Scarboro. The homes share a similar character: mature lots, established trees, and garages that reward a made-to-measure approach rather than a big-box kit.
